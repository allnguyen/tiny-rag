Reranking is a second-stage retrieval process that refines an initial set of retrieved documents. After a fast retrieval step (BM25 or dense search), a more accurate but slower model is used to reorder results.

Rerankers often use cross-encoders that jointly encode query-document pairs to compute relevance scores.

This improves retrieval quality by capturing finer-grained semantic relationships than initial embedding-based search.

Reranking is commonly used in production systems to balance speed and accuracy.

It is especially useful when the first-stage retriever returns a large candidate set with mixed relevance.
