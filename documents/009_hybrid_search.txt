Hybrid search combines lexical retrieval methods like BM25 with dense retrieval methods based on embeddings. The goal is to leverage the strengths of both approaches.

BM25 is strong at exact keyword matching, while dense retrieval is strong at semantic similarity.

Hybrid systems typically combine scores from both methods using weighted sums or merge ranked lists.

This approach improves robustness across different query types, including both keyword-heavy and paraphrased queries.

Hybrid search is widely used in production RAG systems because it provides better coverage and reduces failure cases of single-method retrieval.
