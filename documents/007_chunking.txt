Chunking is the process of splitting long documents into smaller segments before generating embeddings. This is necessary because embedding models have token limits and because smaller units improve retrieval precision.

Common chunking strategies include fixed-size token splitting, sentence-based splitting, and structure-aware splitting using headings or markdown.

Chunk size affects retrieval quality: small chunks improve precision but may lose context, while large chunks preserve context but reduce specificity.

Overlapping chunks are often used to prevent important information from being split across boundaries.

Chunking is a critical design decision in RAG systems and strongly influences downstream retrieval performance.
