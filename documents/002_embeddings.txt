Embeddings are dense vector representations of text where semantic meaning is encoded in a continuous numerical space. Each piece of text is mapped to a high-dimensional vector, typically produced by neural networks.

The key idea is that semantically similar texts are close together in vector space, measured using similarity metrics such as cosine similarity.

For example, “car” and “automobile” would have similar embeddings even if they share no exact words.

Embedding models are trained on large corpora using objectives that encourage semantic similarity to be reflected geometrically.

Embeddings are used in search, clustering, classification, and retrieval systems. In RAG systems, embeddings allow queries and documents to be compared based on meaning rather than exact keyword overlap.
